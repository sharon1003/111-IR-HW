lesson bfigurative language figurative language is words or groups of words that stand for more than their literal meaning a simile is one kind of figurative language a simile uses the words like or as to compare two unlike things for example an author might say tiger crept up behind fox as silently as a whisper the author is comparing the way tiger crept to a whisper to show that tiger crept very quietly a metaphor is another kind of figurative language a metaphor also compares two unlike things but does not use like or as for example an author might say the monkeys were statues frozen in their trees the author is comparing the monkeys to statues to show how still they were figurative language can be unexpected and tricky to figure out to learn how to determine the meaning of these two kinds of figurative language click the links below determine the meaning of similes determining the meaning of metaphors
