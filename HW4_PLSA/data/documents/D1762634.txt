this definition busy signal a busy signal in telephony is an audible or visual signal to the calling party that indicates failure to complete the requested connection of that particular telephone call there are several distinctly different types of busy signals ⁕a reorder tone indicates that no transmission path to the called number is available it is often played after a recording describing a telephone problem ⁕an otherwise unspecified busy signal indicates that the called number is occupied if that number is calling out if someone else has called the number or is calling the number at the same time if the other line was left offhook or it is otherwise unavailable ⁕this tone sometimes occurs at the end of a call to indicate the other party has hung up see disconnect supervision countries have different signaling tones that act as busy signals in most cases consisting of a tone with equal onoff periods at a rate of between 60 and 120 interruptions per minute in north america the precise tone plan used today employs two tones of 480 and 620 hz at 60 ipm in the past before the adoption of the precise tone system busy signal was generally composed of the same tone as dial
